About

Who can apply
Bachelor's, Master's, PhD students and industry persons with computer science, electrical, maths or any other domain related to machine learning and artificial intelligence backgrounds in any year of their study are eligible to apply for this program. It will be held in both virtual and onsite formats.
Experience needed
Participation does not require any previous research experience. Still, having a background in programming languages such as C/C++/Python/Java and completing introductory computer science coursework can be advantageous.
Workshop activities
During this workshop, participants will be teamed up with one or two faculty mentors, who will provide continuous guidance throughout the workshop and beyond. The workshop will encompass a broad spectrum of research endeavours, including a foundational understanding of AI, privacy, and zero-touch networks, familiarity with the current state of the art, collaboration with faculty on existing laboratory projects, and the initiation of new independent research initiatives. After the onsite event concludes, participants will have the opportunity to showcase their work through poster presentations, engage in interactive sessions, explore research laboratory facilities, and participate in hands-on activities. Moreover, there is the potential for extending and publishing these projects as short or full papers.
